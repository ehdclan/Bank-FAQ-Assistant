WEMA BANK & ALAT FAQs (November 2025)

1. GENERAL ALAT & ACCOUNT
   • What is ALAT?  
     ALAT is Wema Bank’s fully digital banking platform. All banking services (account opening, transfers, bills, loans, cards) are available 100% online without visiting a branch.
   • Who can use ALAT?  
     Anyone with a smartphone and valid Nigerian ID.
   • Cost to sign up?  
     Completely free.
   • Supported platforms?  
     Android and iOS (download from Google Play or App Store).
   • How to open an account?  
     Download app → Enter BVN → Upload valid ID (NIN, Driver’s License, or International Passport) → Complete verification.
   • How to create a savings goal?  
     App → Savings/Goals → Create Goal → Choose Fixed (no withdrawals), Flexible (limited access), or Target → Set amount & frequency.
   • How to pay bills?  
     App → Airtime & Bills → Bill Payment → Select provider → Enter customer ID → Confirm with PIN.
   • How to request a debit card?  
     App → Cards → Choose design → Enter delivery address → Confirm with PIN (delivery in 2 business days).
   • How to change password?  
     Settings → Security → Change Password → Enter old → Set new (8+ characters, mix of letters/numbers/symbols).
   • Other ways to bank?  
     Use *945# USSD for basic transactions if no internet.

2. LOANS
   • What is ALAT Salary Loan?  
     Unsecured loan up to ₦4M for salaried employees whose employers are enlisted with Wema/ALAT.
   • Can business owners apply?  
     No — explore SME or personal loans instead.
   • Min/Max amount??
     ₦50,000 – ₦4,000,000 (depends on salary & credit score).
   • Need a Wema account?  
     Yes, but you can open one instantly in the app.
   • Collateral required?  
     No.
   • How is eligibility checked?  
     Instant credit check via lending partners using BVN history, income proof, and risk score.

3. CARDS & VIRTUAL CARDS
   • Lost card — how to block?  
     App → Cards → Select card → Block Card OR call 07000PURPLE immediately.
   • Card not working on POS?  
     Check balance, ensure chip/contactless enabled, or retry later (common: insufficient funds or network glitch).
   • “Issuer offline” error?  
     Temporary bank network issue; retry after 5–10 minutes.
   • What is Virtual Dollar Card?  
     Non-physical USD card for online purchases only. Created in app, funded by converting Naira at bank rate; unused funds can be converted back.
   • Can I transfer money with Virtual Dollar Card?  
     No — online purchases only (no ATM, POS, or transfers).
   • Where accepted?  
     Any website/app accepting dollar cards (except those requiring 3D Secure).
   • Can non-customers get prepaid card?  
     No — open ALAT account first.
   • Can Wema prepaid card be used abroad?  
     Yes, wherever Visa/Mastercard is accepted.

4. ALAT FOR BUSINESS
   • What is it?  
     Corporate internet banking for SMEs & corporates to manage accounts, payments, and finances online.
   • Who can sign up?  
     Any registered business with a Wema account.
   • How to sign up?  
     Visit business.alat.ng → Sign-up → Choose single/multiple signatory → Enter account number → Verify with OTP.
   • Forgot password?  
     business.alat.ng → Forgot Password → Enter username → Reset.
   • View recent transactions?  
     Dashboard → Select account → Recent Activities or Transaction History.
   • Request business debit card?  
     Yes, via Cards section.
   • Offshore transfers?  
     Yes, up to CBN limits ($10,000 per transaction, $50,000 monthly for eligible accounts).
   • What is OUTLET BY ALAT?  
     E-commerce storefront for ALAT for Business users to sell online.
   • Must I have ALAT account to transact on OUTLET?  
     Yes.

5. SECURITY & SUPPORT
   • Is my money insured?  
     Yes — every deposit is insured by NDIC.
   • Security measures?  
     PCI-DSS certified, 24/7 fraud monitoring, AI screening, multi-factor authentication (OTP + PIN).
   • Suspect fraud?  
     Report immediately via app (use different device), call helpline, or email. Account frozen until cleared; no liability if reported promptly.
